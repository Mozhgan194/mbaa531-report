Tariff Ceilings Leave Wide Room Above Applied Rates

Across country-product groups, WTO tariff ceilings typically sit above applied MFN rates, while higher-rate groups also show wider bound-tariff distributions.

By Mozhgan G.

The Data

This report examines 3,366 country-product group observations from a tariff dataset. Each row represents one country and one WTO product group. The key measures are mfn_avg, the average applied most-favoured-nation tariff, and bound_avg, the average WTO bound tariff ceiling. Applied rates show the duties currently used, while bound rates show the maximum commitments recorded in the dataset. Missing values are removed only when a chart requires them. For the ridge chart, values above 75 are excluded so the comparison focuses on the main body of the distributions.

18.3 percentage points: the median gap between the WTO bound tariff ceiling and the applied MFN tariff rate.

A Closer Look

The scatter plot compares applied MFN average tariffs with bound average tariffs. Most observations cluster at low applied rates, especially below about 20, while bound rates vary much more widely. The upward pattern suggests that country-product groups with higher applied tariffs often also have higher tariff ceilings, although the spread is broad and several observations stand out as outliers.

Across Tariff Groups

The ridge chart groups bound_avg by cat_final, the ordered Low, Mid, and High categories created from mfn_avg in Test 3. The Low group is concentrated mainly around 5–15, the Mid group covers a wider range, and the High group shifts farther to the right. Missing values and bound-tariff observations above 75 are excluded so the central differences remain readable.

What This Means

The typical country-product group has meaningful room between its applied MFN tariff and its WTO bound ceiling. The 18.3 percentage-point median duty gap shows that current rates often sit well below the maximum commitments recorded in the data.

The charts also show why a single average is not enough. Higher MFN tariff groups tend to have wider and more right-shifted bound-tariff distributions. A decision-maker comparing sectors or negotiating priorities should look beyond the overall median and identify the product groups where both applied rates and tariff ceilings remain high.
